# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The customer-facing website is for Tucson-area homeowners, property managers, and commercial decision makers who need window cleaning, pressure washing, hard-water removal, or recurring exterior cleaning. They are usually trying to answer three questions quickly: whether Squeegee Guy serves their area, whether the work feels trustworthy, and how easily they can get a quote or book a visit.

## Product Purpose

The website exists to turn local service intent into qualified quote requests and booked jobs. It should make Squeegee Guy feel like a reliable local operator: easy to contact, clear about services, credible in Tucson conditions, and quick to move from inquiry to appointment without phone tag.

Success looks like more visitors completing the quote form, calling from mobile, or emailing with enough detail to receive useful appointment options. The lead-generation and booking tooling behind the site is implementation support; future design work in this project should stay scoped to the public website unless explicitly redirected.

## Brand Personality

Local, reliable, quick, and no-pressure. The voice should feel like a capable neighborhood pro who shows up, knows Tucson glass and hard water, and makes the next step simple. Copy should be direct, practical, and reassuring rather than slick or overproduced.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic national-franchise polish, fake urgency, spammy local SEO pages, exaggerated luxury styling, cheesy clip-art service graphics, and AI-looking landing-page scaffolds. The site should not feel like a template with swapped business names, an aggressive upsell funnel, or a contractor page overloaded with badges and unsupported claims.

## Design Principles

Lead with local trust: Tucson-specific service knowledge should be visible without turning the page into keyword stuffing.

Make the next step obvious: every major section should help a visitor decide whether to call, request a quote, or keep scanning.

Keep proof concrete: use service areas, job types, process details, reviews, and response expectations rather than vague promises.

Reduce friction without pressure: fast quote and booking cues should feel helpful, not pushy.

Preserve the working brand: future iterations should respect the existing blue, amber, and navy identity unless the user explicitly asks for a larger redesign.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for color contrast, keyboard access, readable text sizing, form labels, and focus states. Preserve reduced-motion alternatives for any animation. The site should work well for mobile visitors outdoors or in bright conditions, where tap targets, contrast, and quick phone access matter.
